Pseudo-Classes vs Pseudo-Elements

Pseudo itself means false, unreal, or fake. The prefix pseudo-, is used to reference classes or elements that are not "real".

What are Pseudo-Classes in CSS?

A pseudo-class is a selector that selects elements that are in a specific state, e.g. they are the first element of their type, or they are being hovered over by the mouse pointer. They tend to act as if you had applied a class to some part of your document.

What are Pseudo-Elements in CSS?

Pseudo-elements behave in a similar way. However, they act as if you had added a whole new HTML element into the markup, rather than applying a class to existing elements.

The Difference between Pseudo-classes and Pseudo-elements in CSS

  • As a takeaway, remember that there is a key difference between a single and double colon. Most importantly that : refers to pseudo-classes and :: refers to pseudo-elements.
  • Pseudo-classes are used to target state. Pseudo-elements are used to target specific parts of an element.
